12 · Market Gaps

Three credible gaps, one caution

Gap 1 — Ingredient Story

Real fruit + full mineral spectrum, together

Evidence: REDMOND owns "real salt" alone; LMNT/Ultima/THORNE lead with "zero sugar / keto"; none foreground freeze-dried real fruit plus an 85+ mineral claim as the primary hook. Amazon's own OEI return-reason data adds hard evidence: Artificial Sweeteners (8.55%) and Salt Content (11.84%) are top-3 return drivers niche-wide — exactly what Purest's formulation avoids.

Why it's unfilled: The category's biggest players built brand equity around sugar-free/keto positioning years ago and would cannibalize that equity by repositioning around "whole-food minerals."

Fit: Strong — this is already Purest's core DTC message, and it's now the best-evidenced gap in this report.

Gap 2 — Trial Format

Sub-10-serving trial SKU

Evidence: Only 2/20 kept ASINs sit at ≤10 servings; the category defaults to 16–32-serving commitment purchases.

Why it's unfilled: FBA fees are largely fixed per unit regardless of pack size, so bulk packs give incumbents better margin per shipment — small trial packs are structurally less profitable on Amazon, which is likely why big brands under-invest here.

Fit: Good, but margin-sensitive — the $15.99 5-Day Kit would carry a proportionally heavier fee burden than the jars.

Gap 3 — Bundle Format

Hydration + reusable glass bottle kit

Evidence: No kept competitor bundles a reusable vessel with the powder; Purest's DTC $49 "Hydration Ritual Kit" (jar + 5-day trial + glass bottle) has no Amazon-side analog.

Why it's unfilled: Bundling adds fulfillment complexity (multi-component listings, bundle FBA rules) that smaller/challenger brands are often more willing to absorb than scaled incumbents.

Fit: Good for AOV and differentiation, but adds listing/inventory complexity for a first Amazon launch.

Caution — Not a gap

"No Purest listing in this X-Ray" ≠ "no competition"

Purest's absence from the top-20 by "electrolyte powder" simply means it hasn't built Amazon sales/reviews yet — not that demand is uncontested. All 20 kept competitors are established, well-reviewed brands actively defending this exact keyword.
